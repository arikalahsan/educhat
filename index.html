Grammar Explanations

Clear, detailed explanations of English grammar concepts with multiple examples.

Present Simple Tense

When to Use:

The present simple tense is used for:

  • Habits and routines (I drink coffee every morning)
  • Permanent situations (She works in a bank)
  • General truths (Water boils at 100Â°C)
  • Fixed arrangements (The train leaves at 6 pm)

Structure:

Positive: Subject + base form of verb (add -s/-es for he/she/it)

Negative: Subject + do/does + not + base form

Question: Do/Does + subject + base form

Examples:

Correct: "She works at a hospital." (Third person singular adds -s)

Correct: "They work at a hospital." (Plural subject uses base form)

Incorrect: "She work at a hospital." (Missing -s for third person singular)

Common Mistakes:

  • Forgetting the -s/-es ending for third person singular
  • Using present simple for actions happening now (should use present continuous)
  • Incorrect auxiliary verbs in negatives/questions

Present Continuous Tense

When to Use:

The present continuous tense is used for:

  • Actions happening now (I am reading a book)
  • Temporary situations (She is staying with friends this week)
  • Future arrangements (We are flying to Paris tomorrow)
  • Changing situations (The climate is getting warmer)

Structure:

Positive: Subject + am/is/are + verb-ing

Negative: Subject + am/is/are + not + verb-ing

Question: Am/Is/Are + subject + verb-ing

Examples:

Correct: "They are watching TV right now." (Action in progress)

Correct: "I am meeting John later." (Future arrangement)

Incorrect: "She is work at the moment." (Should be "working")

Common Mistakes:

  • Forgetting the -ing ending on the main verb
  • Using present continuous for permanent situations
  • Confusing with present simple for future events

Articles (a, an, the)

When to Use:

Articles are used before nouns:

  • a/an - indefinite articles (first mention, non-specific)
  • the - definite article (specific or previously mentioned)
  • no article - with plural/uncountable nouns in general sense

Rules:

'a' before consonant sounds (a book, a university)

'an' before vowel sounds (an apple, an hour)

'the' when the listener knows which specific thing you mean

Examples:

Correct: "I saw a dog in the park." (First mention uses 'a', specific park uses 'the')

Correct: "The dog was chasing a cat." (Now we know which dog)

Incorrect: "I want to buy the car." (Which car? Should be 'a car' if not specific)

Common Mistakes:

  • Using 'the' with general plural/uncountable nouns
  • Using 'a' before vowel sounds (should be 'an')
  • Omitting articles with singular countable nouns
